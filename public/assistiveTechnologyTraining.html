Empowering Productive Careers Through Assistive Technology Training

Welcome to the Assistive Technology (AT) training hub of Blind Professionals Network (BPN)! We understand that proficiency in assistive technology is not just about daily independence; it's a critical gateway to meaningful employment and sustained productivity in today's workforce. Our comprehensive AT training programs are meticulously designed to equip blind and visually impaired individuals with the essential digital skills needed to excel in any professional environment.

Whether you're a complete beginner eager to learn the fundamentals or an experienced user looking to refresh and advance your skills, our training program will provide the practical knowledge and confidence you need to thrive in the workplace.

We offer flexible and personalized training in screen readers and a wide range of assistive devices through various formats to suit your learning style and specific employment goals:

Training Format

  • Training Courses: Our structured training courses provide an in-depth and progressive learning experience, covering specific AT topics from foundational concepts to advanced workplace applications. These courses feature detailed modules, hands-on exercises, and opportunities for collaborative learning, ensuring a thorough understanding of each technology.
  • Workshops: Dive into focused, intensive learning with our specialized workshops. These shorter, concentrated sessions are perfect for mastering specific AT features, tackling common workplace challenges, exploring new software functionalities, or quickly getting up to speed on industry-standard tools. Workshops emphasize practical application and direct interaction with expert instructors.
  • 1-on-1 Coaching: For the most personalized and targeted instruction, our 1-on-1 coaching sessions are ideal. A dedicated AT specialist will work with you individually to address your unique learning needs, career aspirations, and the specific assistive technologies relevant to your job function. This customized approach ensures highly effective training that directly translates to enhanced workplace success.

Detailed Training Topics for Workplace Success:

Screen Readers

Gain expert proficiency in screen reader software, enabling seamless access and interaction with digital content essential for virtually any modern job role.

  • Fundamentals for Professional Use:

    • Mastering screen reader navigation across operating systems (Windows, macOS) for efficient computer control.
    • Optimizing speech output settings for clarity and speed in a professional context.
    • Core keyboard commands for rapid execution of tasks.

  • Industry-Standard Screen Reader Proficiency:

    • JAWS (Job Access With Speech): Comprehensive training for navigating Windows-based professional applications, including Microsoft Office Suite (Word for document creation, Excel for data analysis, PowerPoint for presentations, Outlook for email and calendar management), web browsers, and specialized industry software.
    • NVDA (NonVisual Desktop Access): In-depth instruction on this powerful and free screen reader for Windows, focusing on its efficiency for web navigation, document editing, and general business software. Emphasizing customization for peak productivity.
    • VoiceOver (macOS, iOS, iPadOS): Mastering VoiceOver gestures and commands for Apple devices, crucial for professionals using Apple ecosystems. Topics include navigating complex interfaces, utilizing productivity apps, and accessing web-based platforms.
    • TalkBack (Android): Training on Android device navigation, business app usage, and accessibility settings for smartphones and tablets, vital for mobile productivity.

  • Advanced Workplace Screen Reader Techniques:

    • Efficiently reading and navigating complex data tables, spreadsheets, and databases.
    • Seamlessly filling out online forms and applications.
    • Collaborating on shared documents and platforms.
    • Troubleshooting screen reader compatibility with enterprise software.
    • Utilizing virtual cursors and object navigation for precise control.

Assistive Devices for Professional Productivity:

Learn to effectively integrate a range of assistive devices to enhance your performance and independence in the workplace.

  • Braille Displays and Notetakers:

    • Connecting and configuring refreshable braille displays for real-time braille output from computers and mobile devices.
    • Efficiently reading and navigating digital documents, emails, and presentations in braille.
    • Utilizing braille notetakers for on-the-go information management, meeting notes, and quick document creation.
    • Mastering braille input methods for rapid text entry.

  • Screen Magnification Software (for Low Vision Professionals):

    • Expert training on software like ZoomText, Fusion, and built-in magnifiers.
    • Optimizing magnification levels, color schemes, and pointers for specific tasks and reducing eye strain during prolonged computer use.
    • Seamlessly integrating magnification with screen readers for a hybrid approach.

  • Optical Character Recognition (OCR) Systems for Document Access:

    • Using dedicated OCR devices and software to convert printed documents (e.g., reports, invoices, physical mail) into digital, searchable, and screen reader-accessible formats.
    • Leveraging mobile OCR apps for quick scanning of labels, business cards, and other immediate text.

  • Smartphones and Tablets in the Professional Environment:

    • Maximizing built-in accessibility features (e.g., voice assistants, dictation, display adjustments) for everyday work tasks.
    • Identifying and utilizing accessibility-focused apps for organization, communication, and industry-specific functions.
